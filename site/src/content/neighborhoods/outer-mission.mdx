Outer Mission sits at San Francisco's southern edge, a residential neighborhood bordered by the Mission District to the north and Daly City to the south. Home to roughly 5,300 residents, it carries all three equity designations: Analysis Neighborhood, Environmental Justice Community, and SFMTA Equity Strategy area. That triple designation often signals a neighborhood that transit planning has underserved.

The data tells a more nuanced story. At the default settings -- a bus every 6 minutes or better, within a 3-minute walk -- about 49% of Outer Mission residents have access to frequent service. That figure is surprisingly strong for a peripheral, lower-income neighborhood. Widen the walking threshold to 5 minutes and the share jumps to 82%. The Mission Street corridor, with its dense bus service, anchors much of this coverage, pulling the southern blocks into reach even under strict frequency definitions.

The counterintuitive finding here is one of degree: Outer Mission's transit access outperforms several wealthier, more central neighborhoods when measured by the same yardstick. The equity designations suggest a neighborhood in need -- and it is, on many dimensions -- but on the specific question of walking distance to frequent buses, the picture is more favorable than a casual assumption would predict.
